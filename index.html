Product Manager Who Actually Builds Things Shocks Absolutely Nobody Who Knows Him

Entrepreneurial technologist combines strategic vision with hands-on execution; refuses to "just make a deck about it"
By The Abhishek Times Staff · Front Page

Abhishek is a product manager who likes being close to the work: strategy, systems thinking, execution, and the occasional prototype when words are no longer enough. He sits comfortably between product and engineering, which is usually where the useful conversations happen.

He writes clearly, thinks commercially, and enjoys turning fuzzy ideas into something shippable. Also plays a lot of tennis, which explains the optimism.

What He Actually Does

Product Strategy & Execution

Roadmaps rooted in user problems, clear trade-offs, and practical delivery. Strong on discovery, prioritisation, and turning intent into shipped work.

The Boring Stuff

Backlogs, release notes, sprint rituals, stakeholder comms, and the admin that keeps teams moving.

Technical Depth

Comfortable in architecture, API, and data conversations. Technical enough to ask sharper questions and spot risk early.

Builder Mode

Can prototype ideas directly. This site is intentionally simple: static HTML, CSS, and a bit of stubbornness.

The Entrepreneurial Bit

Thinks with a founder's bias toward speed, clarity, and commercial reality. Interested in product-market fit, pricing, and useful MVPs.

Communication

Clear writing, tidy meetings, and follow-through. Communication is treated as part of the job, not theatre around it.
